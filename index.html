About

I’m a BSE Mechanical Engineering Systems student at Arizona State University, passionate about building human-centered technologies that bridge engineering and empathy. My work focuses on assistive design, sensor integration, and interdisciplinary collaboration across engineering and design.

Current Project: Rehab Companion Light

Rehab Companion Light is a wearable assistive-tech device that helps rehabilitation patients visualize their progress through responsive light feedback. The system detects force and movement using pressure and motion sensors, translating data into intuitive LED signals that motivate and track daily improvements.

The project began during my first Delta Program residency and has evolved through mentorship and lab collaboration at ASU. My next goal is to create a flexible patch version integrating micro-sensors and Bluetooth connectivity for real-time progress tracking.

Focus Areas: Sensor fusion · Rapid prototyping · Embedded systems · Human-centered design
